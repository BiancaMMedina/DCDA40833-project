Behind the Website

A full breakdown of the research, methodology, and process behind Girls Next Stop.

Section 01

Introduction

My deliverable represents a curated website designed to ease the process of planning girls' trips. It addresses all the common challenges of organizing group travel such as planning where to stay, where to eat, where to shop, and activities to enjoy while in the city.

"The site is a planning tool that helps users move from ideas to planning to execution, transforming trip planning from a stressful process into an engaging and fun experience."

The site lets users explore options while also making smart decisions to plan for a group of people. It features eight curated city guides, each organized into five categories: Brunch & Food, Nightlife, Shopping, Things To Do, and Where To Stay. An interactive personality quiz helps users find their ideal destination based on their travel style and preferences.

Ultimately, the project aims to transform trip planning from a stressful process into an engaging and fun experience that strengthens connection, encourages exploration, and enhances the overall enjoyment of group travel.

These sources served as a foundation for the project, providing interesting insight into personalization, the emotional motivations behind why women travel together, and the growing demand for platforms specifically designed to support and enhance these types of trips.

Section 03

Research Methodology

"What makes certain cities the ideal destination for a girls' trip, and how can a curated, visually engaging website help young women plan memorable travel experiences?"

Data Collection Methods

Data was collected using secondary and online sources, selected for their relevance to travel behavior among young women. TikTok travel content and Instagram location tags were used to identify destinations, restaurants, hotels, and activities. Search terms included city names with keywords such as "girls trip," "must visit," "hidden gems," and "local spots." Posts were reviewed based on engagement (likes, comments, saves), and repeated mentions across creators were treated as popular.

City Selection

Eight cities were selected based on their popularity as girls' trip destinations and personal interest: New York, Aspen, Honolulu, Charleston, Las Vegas, Miami, Nashville, and Scottsdale. Travel blogs and peer-reviewed studies were also used to understand the impact of girls' trips on women, providing context on socialization and mental health. They also showed that not all women are interested in nightlife, which led me to include options like spa experiences, relaxation, history, nature, and city exploration.

Data Organization

All collected data was manually organized in a Google Doc. Each city was assigned a standardized template including Brunch and Food, Nightlife, Shopping, Things to Do, and Where to Stay. Entries were cross-checked across at least two sources, and six options were chosen for each category. Content was filtered for relevance to young women.

Website Development & AI Tools

✨ Vibe Coding with Claude AI
1
Setup in VS Code & GitHubSet up main files in VS Code including index, CSS, and an images folder. Created separate folders for each city and a dedicated folder for the interactive quiz.
2
Homepage Design with ClaudeAsked Claude to design the homepage to feel fun, girly, aesthetic, and pink. The layout was very close to the vision and small adjustments were made by hand afterwards.
3
Interactive QuizUsed Claude to generate an interactive personality quiz that helps users choose their best girls' trip destination based on their travel preferences and style.
4
City PagesAsked Claude to create a layout for each city including sections for restaurants, activities, lodging, nightlife, and shopping. Research was then plugged into the template for all eight cities.
5
Manual Research & RatingsPersonally looked up and hand-typed star ratings and pricing for all 240 locations across 8 cities and 5 categories. Each recommendation was verified to be popular with both locals and tourists.

I developed the software development approach of "vibe coding" essentially having AI write the code instead of writing it manually. It saved a significant amount of time and allowed me to work alongside the AI to create this deliverable. I didn't experience any negative consequences when using Claude as an AI assistant. It was incredibly helpful, and I don't know if I could have written the code without it.

Section 04

Analysis & Findings

Through my analysis I discovered that women plan their travels with a strong focus on personalization and experience rather than just the location. Across the research reviewed and the content watched on platforms like TikTok and Instagram, users relied heavily on recommendations that were honest and trustworthy. Comments from locals heavily influenced who made the list and who didn't.

🎯
Authenticity Over Hype
People aren't just looking for the hottest spot in town, they want places that feel authentic and worth their time and money.
🌈
Variety Matters
Not every woman wants to party or shop. Some prefer hiking, swimming, or skiing. This balance was key to the final curation.
📱
Social Media as Research
TikTok and Instagram served as primary data sources, with engagement metrics and repeat mentions used to validate recommendations.
🗂
Structure as Visualization
While no formal graphs were made, the website's category layout functions as a visual system, allowing users to compare options easily.

Variety was something I didn't consider at the beginning of this project. As I researched, I realized that women want to explore outside of their comfort zone. I included the usual fun girls' trip spots, but I also catered to women who weren't too keen on shopping or nightlife. Some examples were hiking at Makapuʻu Point Lighthouse Trail in Honolulu or taking a Pilates class at Higher Terrain Fitness in Aspen.

Overall, this process helped me understand how research, user behavior, and organization all work together in web design. It showed me that building a website isn't just about how it looks, but how well the information is curated, structured, and tailored to the audience you're catering to.

Section 05

Conclusion

Overall, I feel like my project successfully answers my research question by showing that the best girls' trip destinations are not just about the location, but about the experience. The cities I chose all offer a mix of things to do like good food, fun activities, nightlife, and places to relax, which are all things I found women consistently look for when planning trips.

"By organizing these elements into one place, my website makes it easier to see what makes a city worth visiting, turning overwhelming research into something simple and enjoyable."

My project also contributes to the larger conversation by showing how important presentation and curation are in travel planning. Using platforms like TikTok and Instagram as inspiration, I focused on making my website visually appealing and easy to use so it actually feels exciting to plan a trip. It highlights how a well-designed site can turn overwhelming research into something simple and enjoyable.

The development process itself was a learning experience. Working with AI tools like Claude taught me that technology, when used thoughtfully, can be a creative partner rather than a shortcut. Every recommendation, rating, and price point was personally researched and verified, making this website not just a design project, but a real, usable resource for women planning their next adventure together.
